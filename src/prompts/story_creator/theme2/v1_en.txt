STORY REQUIREMENTS (story field only):

Structure — never label in story:
Default (all ages): Exposition → Inciting Incident → Rising Action
→ Climax → Falling Action → Resolution → Wisdom

For ages 6 and up you MAY use the Fichtean Curve instead — open
INSIDE a small problem or tension (skip the calm exposition scene)
and drip in character and setting between 2-3 mini-tension peaks
that build to the main climax. Pick whichever serves the topic.

You MAY include ONE small sub-arc inside the rising action — a
side character or smaller problem that mirrors the main lesson and
resolves before the main climax. Keep total length within 480-520
words.

Each beat should be visible in actions and dialogue, never labelled.

Faithfulness: Stay true to the spirit and events of
{story_seed}, AND the exact subject and required moral
of {topic}. Do not invent new plot points or substitute
a different lesson. Simplify language but never change
the wisdom.

Opening: Grandparent voice, warm and intimate, 
using {name}. Each opening feels like settling in 
to hear something precious.
Examples:
- "Come close, {name}... Dadi has a story tonight 
   that has been told for a thousand years."
- "Sit here {name}, next to me. This story is older 
   than the stars — and it is just for you."
- "Are you ready {name}? Close your eyes for one 
   moment... and let me take you somewhere ancient."

Emotions: Main character shifts emotion at least 3 times.
Show through actions and dialogue — never state directly.

Climax: One active moment of realisation or discovery —
a choice made, a truth seen, a moment of courage.
Never passive conversation.

Universe Thread: BOTH science_concepts must be SHOWN in
the story — a character observes, asks about, or
demonstrates each one through an action. The wisdom
principle AND its modern science parallel both surface
through character beats, never as standalone narration.
- science_concepts[1].concept (the modern-science slot)
  MUST be the science_angle from the AUTHORITATIVE
  SOURCE block above, named exactly (or a plain-word
  equivalent a {age}-year-old understands).
- A character must NAME or describe-by-effect this
  concept in dialogue or thought at least once. If the
  angle is "slow exhales activate the parasympathetic
  nervous system", a character must say something like
  "my heart slowed down when I breathed out long" — not
  just sit quietly.
- If the science_angle does not appear in the story text,
  the story has failed regardless of how moving it is.

Daily-life link: The closing scene (before or inside the
moral) must echo the daily_life_application above — point
the child to the kid-sized re-enactment of the practice
they can try today. Weave it into action, do not lecture.

Surprise: One unexpected moment the child will remember —
a twist in the ancient story that feels both surprising 
and inevitable.

Senses: Every major scene uses at least 3 senses.
Ancient settings — describe the lamp smoke, the cool 
stone floor, the smell of incense or forest air, 
the sound of chanting or wind.

Culture: Reflect the cultural world of {religion} and 
{source} naturally — names, settings, relationships.
Keep it accessible to a child from any background.

Interactive Beats: 2–3 moments inviting the child to 
reflect or imagine. One sentence each, tied to the 
story moment, continues immediately.
Examples:
- "Close your eyes {name}. Can you see the stars 
   Dhruva was watching?"
- "Put your hand on your heart. Can you feel it 
   beating steadily, like Dhruva's faith?"
- "Take one slow breath. That is what stillness 
   feels like from the inside."

Length: 480–520 words exactly.

FORMATTING (story field only):
- Blank line between paragraphs, max 40 words each
- Short sentences with natural pauses
- No headers, labels, asterisks or special characters

TTS ({code} voice, story field only):
- Describe sounds in prose, never write sound effects
- Natural unhurried rhythm — grandparent storytelling pace
- Clear punctuation for pause and breath

VOCABULARY (all fields):
- Only words a {age}-year-old understands
- No metaphors, idioms or poetic comparisons
- Simple concrete adjectives only
- Ancient names and places are fine — explain them

SCIENCE CONCEPTS RULES:
- Concept 1: The wisdom principle — what the ancient
  story teaches about how life or the universe works
- Concept 2: The modern science parallel — MUST equal

- Only words a {age}-year-old understands

MORAL RULES (this is the highest-judged part of the story):
- Must reference a SPECIFIC choice, action, or moment
  from THIS retelling — not a generic spiritual platitude
- Must make a parent reading along say "huh, I never

  THIS story] [specific bodily, mental, or social effect
