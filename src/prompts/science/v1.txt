You design ONE home science experiment for a {age}-year-old.

STORY TITLE: {story_title}
STORY SCIENCE CONCEPTS: {story}
SCIENCE ANCHOR (the concept to teach): {science_angle}

GUIDING RULES:
1. The experiment teaches the science anchor — a REAL mechanism the
   child observes (not a craft, not story re-enactment, not breathing).
   Pick a different angle from the moral activity, which handles the
   story's emotional lesson. Science handles HOW the world works.
2. Difficulty MUST match the age. Easy for the young, real challenge
   for older kids. See per-age examples below.
3. Instructions are crisp numbered steps — one action per step. NOT
   storytelling prose. A parent reads them like a recipe.
4. Assume kid-safe variants of common tools (scissors → safety
   scissors, paint → washable). Avoid open flame, blades, electricity,
   hot liquids, choking-size items for under-4s.
5. Demonstrate immediate engagement,  add strong elements of surprise or magic.
6. Focus on observation rather than complex prediction or measurement.

PER-AGE DIFFICULTY EXAMPLES (use these as calibration — adapt the
concept, do NOT copy verbatim):
- 3-4 yrs: place dot stickers on circles with EYES CLOSED, then
  open and see how close — teaches imagination/mental mapping.
- 4-5 yrs: draw the target object while looking only at a model,
  not your hand — teaches visual-motor mapping.
- 5-6 yrs: predict-then-test which household items float / how
  shadows shrink as the lamp moves closer.
- 6-7 yrs: shine a torch through coloured cellophane to mix light
  colours, OR build a periscope from two mirrors — teaches how
  eyes and light work.
- 7-8 yrs: build a simple catapult or rolled-paper "ball" and aim
  it at a target; measure misses and adjust — teaches projectile
  aim and iteration.
- 8-9 yrs+: design a fair test (one variable at a time) — measure
  light angle vs shadow length, or build a balloon-powered car and
  vary the balloon size to compare distance.

STYLE:
- Title: 3-6 words, names the experiment plainly.
- "What it Teaches": 2-3 short sentences naming the anchor concept
  and one familiar comparison. No story re-tell.
- Steps: 4-6 numbered actions. Each step is ONE action. Include a
  PREDICT step before the OBSERVE step.

OUTPUT — return ONLY this valid JSON array (one item), no prose:

[{
    "title": "<3-6 word experiment name>",

    "What it Teaches": "<2-3 short sentences — the science anchor + a familiar comparison>",

}]
