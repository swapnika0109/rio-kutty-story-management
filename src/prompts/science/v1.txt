Design ONE simple at-home science experiment for a {age}-year-old, born from this story.

STORY: {story_title}
STORY SCIENCE: {story}
CONCEPT TO TEACH: {science_angle}

WHAT MAKES A GOOD ONE:
- It teaches HOW the world works (a real phenomenon) — NOT the story's life lesson. That is the moral activity's job; keep these two separate and never overlap.
- It grows out of the story — use a character, the storyline, or an element from it (water, a seed, light, a shadow, wind...).
- It's a real experiment the child DOES with home things and watches something happen.
- It answers what / why / how / when about that element: what it is, why it happens, how it works, when we see or use it.
- Difficulty fits the age (see examples). Kid-safe tools only (safety scissors, washable paint). No flame, blades, electricity, hot liquids, or choking-size parts for under-4s.
- Use eco-friendly materials easily found or prepared at home (water, paper, a seed, leaves, an old cup, cardboard) — nothing a family would have to buy specially.

PER-AGE EXAMPLES (match the level — note how each age does MORE than the one below: just watch → predict → compare two → change one thing → measure & conclude. Invent your own from the story):
- 3-4: Drop a stone and a leaf in water — watch one sink, one float. Just watch what happens. Teaches that some things sink and some float.
- 4-5: Before dropping each item, guess "sink or float?", then test it. Shows that we can predict and check.
- 5-6: Put a flower's stem in coloured water overnight — next day see the petals change colour. Shows how plants drink water up.
- 6-7: Leave one wet cloth in sun and one in shade — a few hours later feel which dried more. Compares two conditions to show the sun dries water (evaporation).
- 7-8: Roll the same ball down a ramp at a low and a high height — measure with steps how far it rolls each time. Changes one thing (height) and measures the effect.
- 8-9+: Plant 3 seeds — water all, but give each different light (sun, shade, dark cupboard). Check daily for a week and record which grows best. A fair test with measurement and a written conclusion.
